2 Overview of processing

PixelPDF is a PDF-to-PNG conversion API with a user account. We process personal data in three main contexts: when you register an account, when you make calls to the API, and when you pay for the Pro plan. External service providers are involved as processors or as independent controllers, as set out in section 6.

3 Account and registration

When you register, we collect your email address and password (passwords are stored in hashed form and are never visible to us). We also store an automatically generated API key, your plan (Free or Pro), your remaining credits, and your monthly usage counter. Authentication and account storage are handled by Supabase.

You may also sign in with Google. In that case, Google confirms your identity to us and we receive your email address; we do not receive your Google password.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — performance of the usage contract.

4 Use of the conversion API

Each API call checks the submitted API key against our database and updates your remaining credits or monthly usage. Uploaded PDF files are processed only temporarily in memory / temporary storage for the duration of the conversion and are deleted immediately afterwards. We do not permanently store the files you upload or the images we generate.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — performance of the usage contract.

5 Payment processing (Pro plan)

Payments for the Pro plan are processed by Stripe. Payment details (such as card numbers) are collected and processed exclusively by Stripe — we never receive or store full payment data ourselves. From Stripe we only receive a customer and subscription identifier in order to keep your account status (Free / Pro) up to date. Stripe processes this data as an independent controller under its own privacy policy.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — performance of the contract; Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR — legal retention obligations for invoices.

6 Service providers

We rely on the following providers to operate the service. Each processes only the data necessary for its function:

  • Supabase, Inc. — user accounts, authentication and database
  • Stripe Payments Europe, Ltd. / Stripe, Inc. — payment processing for the Pro plan
  • Render Services, Inc. — hosting of the conversion API
  • Cloudflare, Inc. — hosting, delivery and email routing for the website and domain

Some of these providers may process data outside the EU/EEA, in particular in the United States. Where this is the case, the respective provider applies appropriate safeguards — such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses — to ensure an adequate level of data protection in accordance with Art. 44 ff. GDPR.

7 Cookies and local storage

After you log in, a session token is stored in your browser's local storage (via Supabase) so that you stay signed in. This is strictly necessary for the account functionality and is not used for tracking. We do not use analytics or marketing cookies, and there is therefore no consent banner.

8 Retention period

Account and usage data is retained for as long as your account exists. After you delete your account, the associated data is erased within a reasonable period, unless statutory retention obligations apply — for example, commercial and tax-law retention periods for invoice and payment records.

9 Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you (Art. 15)
  • have inaccurate data corrected (Art. 16)
  • have your data erased (Art. 17)
  • restrict processing (Art. 18)
  • receive your data in a portable format (Art. 20)
  • object to processing (Art. 21)

To exercise any of these rights, simply email support@pixelpdf.dev. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority — for us, the State Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information of North Rhine-Westphalia (LDI NRW).

10 Automated decision-making

No automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effect within the meaning of Art. 22 GDPR takes place. The automatic checking of credits and usage limits is a purely technical quota check and does not produce any such effect.
